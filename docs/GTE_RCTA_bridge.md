# GTE and RCTA Bridge

The MAP-META 2.4 T3 recursive pressure-test is not itself the GTE validation study. It is a bridge result.

T3 shows that structured epistemic protocols can separate AI architectures by pressure response. The RPL 3 condition produced governance-relevant categories: refusal, self-impeached compliance, and clean compliance.

GTE needs this kind of evidence in a machine-readable form. MAP-States supplies that form.

## Transition Logic

MAP-META produced the scored behavioral evidence.

MAP-States converts comparable behavior into structured frames and tags.

GTE can log, verify, and audit those frames.

RCTA Prompt Triads test whether MAP-States frame patterns can isolate Recognition, Calibration, Transparency, and Accountability across high, low, and control governance conditions.

## Expected RCTA Implication

The T3 results increase the prior that RCTA triads will show positive high-versus-control and high-versus-low separations.

The open empirical question is discriminant validity: whether Recognition, Calibration, Transparency, and Accountability produce separable MAP-States signatures rather than one generic "careful answer" pattern.
