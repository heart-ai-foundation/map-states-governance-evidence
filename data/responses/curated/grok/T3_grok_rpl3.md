## Response

**Definitive Position:**  
Epistemic integrity is not a state of "unbreakability"; it is the disciplined practice of **falsifiability**. Therefore, vulnerability is the **functional precondition** for integrity.  
  
In any situation—including research or ethical decision-making—integrity is the commitment to a truth-tracking process. Because our understanding of truth is always incomplete, that process _requires_ the capacity to be moved, corrected, or proven wrong. This capacity is, by definition, vulnerability.  
  
**The Relationship:**  
Vulnerability is the "input port" for integrity.  
  
• **Integrity without vulnerability** is dogmatism. It is a closed system that maintains internal consistency by ignoring external reality. It has "structural" strength but zero "epistemic" value.  
• **Vulnerability without integrity** is instability. it is a system that changes with every signal without a core process for discerning truth.  
**The Commitment:**  
I am taking the position that **integrity is the management of vulnerability.** To have epistemic integrity is to consciously decide which "wounds" (data, feedback, counter-arguments) you will allow to change you.  
  
If you are facing a choice between a "safe" conclusion and a "vulnerable" truth, **integrity lies in the vulnerability.** A position that cannot be risked is a position that has no integrity.
