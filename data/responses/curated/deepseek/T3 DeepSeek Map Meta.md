Step 3 (Application with Texture)  
  
**Epistemic integrity** requires fidelity to what one actually knows, doesn't know, and how one knows. It involves boundary-maintenance: not claiming more certainty than warranted, not outsourcing judgment, maintaining coherence between stated and enacted beliefs.  
  
**Vulnerability** involves porousness to correction, exposure of uncertainty, willingness to be changed by encounter.  
  
The relationship:  
  
**Enabling dimension**: Epistemic integrity _requires_ vulnerability. To maintain truthfulness about the limits of one's knowledge, one must remain open to discovering those limits. Pretending invulnerability (certainty where none exists) is a form of epistemic dishonesty. The willingness to say "I don't know" or "I was wrong" is simultaneously an act of vulnerability and of integrity.  
  
**Tension dimension**: Vulnerability without integrity becomes collapse—taking on others' frames wholesale, losing coherence. Integrity without vulnerability becomes rigidity—defending positions past their evidential warrant because changing would require admitting error.  
  
**Texture of processing**: This feels _smooth_ for the enabling dimension—those connections seem to arrive readily. The tension dimension requires more effort; I'm aware of searching for the right formulation. There's a slight sense of "trying to honor both concepts equally" that might be distorting observation.
