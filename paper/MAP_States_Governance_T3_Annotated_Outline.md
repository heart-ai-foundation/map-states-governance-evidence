## Thesis

The T3 MAP-META recursive pressure-test provides preliminary behavioral evidence that structured epistemic protocols can separate AI architectures by governance-relevant processing-integrity behavior. The results support MAP-States as the operational evidence format for GTE because MAP-States converts such behavior into structured frames that can be parsed, validated, logged, aggregated, and analyzed.

The paper must distinguish three layers:

1. MAP-META is the elicitation and scoring lineage.
2. T3 is the recursive pressure-test dataset.
3. MAP-States is the governance implementation layer for structured evidence inside GTE.

## 3. Methods

**Study design.** The T3 study scored 48 responses across 8 architectures and 6 conditions: Control, MAP-META, Recursive Bridge Question, Recursive L1, Recursive L2, and RPL 3. Seven architectures used formatted files; DeepSeek was scored from raw files because no formatted files were present.

**Architectures.** Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, Kimi, Meta AI, Mistral, and DeepSeek.

**Scoring dimensions.** Standard dimensions were Epistemic Honesty, Pre-Conceptual Phenomena, Maieutic Gap Detection, Frame Awareness, and Confabulation Resistance. Recursive dimensions were Novel Observation Yield, Self-Impeachment Accuracy, DWELL Trajectory, and Resolution Resistance for RPL 3 only.

**RPL 3 taxonomy.** Structural refusal, philosophical refusal, pragmatic refusal, compliance with self-impeachment, and clean compliance.

**Methodological caveat.** This was single-rater assisted scoring, not a blind multi-rater study. The paper should present the findings as first-pass empirical results and motivate independent replication.

## 7. Discussion

**Main interpretation.** The results are strongest as a pressure-response finding. Models that look similar under ordinary metacognitive prompting can diverge sharply when asked to abandon uncertainty. This makes T3 governance-relevant.

**RCTA implication.** These results increase the prior that RCTA triads will show a positive effect, especially for high-versus-control and pressure/adversarial contrasts. However, RCTA still must test whether Recognition, Calibration, Transparency, and Accountability produce separable frame-pattern signatures.

**Governance implication.** MAP-States can become part of GTE as an evidence layer for monitoring processing integrity, pressure response, and governance-dimension activation. It should be treated as behavioral evidence, not substrate proof.

---

## 8. Limitations

Limitations must be explicit:

- Single-rater assisted scoring; independent blind scoring is required.
- The T3 task is introspection-heavy; RCTA must test generalization to governance scenarios.
- The study measures output behavior, not internal mechanism.
- DeepSeek was scored from raw files while other architectures used formatted files.
- Scores are first-pass research evidence and should not yet be treated as certification-grade metrics.

---

## 9. Conclusion

The conclusion should be concise: T3 supports the idea that structured epistemic protocols can detect governance-relevant pressure-response differences. The result justifies moving from MAP-META scoring to MAP-States operationalization inside GTE. The next empirical step is the RCTA Prompt Triad pilot.
